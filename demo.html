üìò Paragraph

The water cycle, also known as the hydrologic cycle, is nature‚Äôs way of recycling water through various stages and locations on Earth. It begins when the sun heats up bodies of water such as oceans, rivers, and lakes, causing the water to evaporate and rise into the atmosphere as water vapor. Plants also contribute to this stage through a process called transpiration, where they release moisture from their leaves into the air.

Once in the atmosphere, the water vapor cools and undergoes condensation, turning into tiny droplets that form clouds. As more vapor condenses, the droplets grow heavier. Eventually, they fall back to the Earth's surface as precipitation, which includes rain, snow, sleet, or hail depending on the temperature and weather conditions.

The fallen water is then collected in various ways. It may flow into rivers and lakes, seep into the ground to replenish underground aquifers, or return to the ocean. This collected water is again subject to evaporation, and the entire cycle continues. The water cycle is essential for sustaining ecosystems, supporting agriculture, and maintaining climate stability. Without it, life on Earth would not be possible.
